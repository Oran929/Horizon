## [Huawei Proposes 'Tao's Law' as New Semiconductor Scaling Principle](https://t.me/zaihuapd/42346) ⭐️ 9.0/10

At the 2026 International Symposium on Circuits and Systems in Shanghai, Huawei announced 'Tao's Law', which replaces traditional geometric scaling with time (τ) scaling to advance semiconductor performance. Over the past six years, Huawei has designed and mass-produced 381 chips based on this principle, and a new Kirin chip using logic folding technology is expected this fall. Tao's Law offers a potential path beyond Moore's Law, which is approaching physical limits, by focusing on reducing time constants rather than transistor size. This could reshape the global semiconductor industry, reduce reliance on extreme ultraviolet lithography, and enable continued performance gains through multi-level optimization. The core of Tao's Law is to systematically reduce a single characteristic time constant τ across devices, circuits, chips, and systems, using techniques like logic folding. Huawei projects that by 2031, high-end chips based on this law could achieve transistor density equivalent to that of a 1.4nm process.

telegram · zaihuapd · Jul 4, 04:56

**Background**: Moore's Law, which predicted that transistor density would double every two years, is slowing due to physical and economic constraints. Traditional geometric scaling shrinks transistor dimensions to improve performance, but this approach is becoming increasingly difficult. Tao's Law proposes a paradigm shift: instead of making transistors smaller, optimize the speed of signal propagation by reducing time constants. This is analogous to improving a computer's performance by reducing latency rather than adding more cores.
